% ABSTRACT
% ============================================================
\begin{abstract}
Selective state space models (SSMs) achieve strong keyword spotting (KWS) accuracy on edge devices, yet a puzzling asymmetry persists: SSMs trained on \emph{clean data only} retain 77.7\% accuracy at 0\,dB SNR---11\,\%p above the best comparable CNN---demonstrating remarkable \emph{structural} noise robustness.
However, after noise-augmented training, CNNs overtake SSMs, reversing this advantage.
We trace this paradox to \textbf{multiplicative noise propagation}: SSM input-dependent parameters ($\Delta$, $\mathbf{B}$, $\mathbf{C}$) create $O(\sigma_n^6)$ output variance versus $O(\sigma_n^2)$ for CNN fixed kernels---a 1{,}000$\times$ gap at $-15$\,dB.
We propose \textbf{SM-SSM} (Selectivity-Modulated SSM), which smoothly interpolates between selective Mamba dynamics and fixed LTI dynamics via an SNR-derived gate $\sigma$, with provable superset guarantee and $O(\sigma_n^6) \to O(\sigma_n^2)$ variance reduction, adding only +38~parameters.
We extend this to \textbf{NC-SSM} (Noise-Conditioned SM-SSM), replacing scalar SNR gating with per-sub-band selectivity and adding a \textbf{Learned Spectral Gate (LSG)} for explicit input denoising.
NC-SSM achieves this with only \textbf{7{,}443~parameters}---21 fewer than the CNN baseline BC-ResNet-1 (7{,}464).
At matched parameters ($\sim$7.4K), NC-SSM ties BC-ResNet-1 clean accuracy (95.3\%) and surpasses it under extreme stationary noise with zero-parameter spectral enhancement.
The decisive advantage is computational: NC-SSM achieves \textbf{2.8$\times$ fewer MACs}, \textbf{2.8$\times$ lower latency} (3.5\,ms vs.\ 9.8\,ms on Cortex-M7), and \textbf{3.2$\times$ less RAM} than BC-ResNet-1, establishing a new efficiency--robustness Pareto front for ultra-compact KWS.
\end{abstract}

\end{IEEEkeywords}


% ============================================================
% I. INTRODUCTION
% ============================================================
\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

\IEEEPARstart{K}{eyword} spotting (KWS) enables always-on voice interfaces on microcontrollers (MCUs) and edge devices, where model size is constrained to sub-256\,KB and inference latency must remain below 10\,ms~\cite{banbury2021mlperf,lin2020mcunet}.
CNN architectures such as DS-CNN~\cite{zhang2017hello} and BC-ResNet~\cite{kim2021bcresnet} dominate this space, achieving 95--96\% accuracy on Google Speech Commands (GSC) V2~\cite{warden2018speech} with 7--24K parameters.
State space models (SSMs)~\cite{gu2024mamba,gu2022efficiently}, particularly the selective SSM architecture Mamba~\cite{gu2024mamba}, offer linear-time sequential modeling with input-dependent dynamics, making them attractive for edge deployment due to constant-memory streaming inference.

A striking and previously unexplained observation motivates this work.
When trained on \textbf{clean data only} and evaluated under noise at 0\,dB SNR, our SSM model (NanoMamba, 7{,}402 parameters) retains \textbf{77.7\%} average accuracy---\textbf{11.0\,\%p} above BC-ResNet-1 (66.7\%, 7{,}464 parameters) and \textbf{24.5\,\%p} above DS-CNN-S (53.2\%, 23{,}756 parameters).
This demonstrates that SSMs possess \emph{structural} noise robustness that CNNs entirely lack: the SSM's input-dependent dynamics ($\Delta$-modulation, $\mathbf{B}$-gating) inherently adapt to noisy inputs without any noise exposure during training.

Yet after noise-augmented training, this advantage \emph{reverses}: BC-ResNet-1 (89.0\%) surpasses NanoMamba (88.3\%), especially at low SNR.
We identify \textbf{multiplicative noise propagation} as the root cause.
SSM input-dependent parameters create a cubic noise coupling $\Delta(x) \cdot B(x) \cdot x$, yielding $O(\sigma_n^6)$ output variance versus $O(\sigma_n^2)$ for CNN fixed kernels---a 1{,}000$\times$ gap at $-15$\,dB.
While SSMs are inherently adaptive, this very adaptivity amplifies noise multiplicatively, negating the structural advantage under aggressive noise augmentation.

We resolve this paradox with two progressive innovations.
First, \textbf{SM-SSM} (Selectivity-Modulated SSM) introduces an SNR-derived gate $\sigma$ that smoothly interpolates between selective Mamba dynamics ($\sigma{=}1$) and fixed LTI dynamics ($\sigma{=}0$, mathematically equivalent to a learned causal convolution), reducing noise variance from $O(\sigma_n^6)$ to $O(\sigma_n^2)$ with provable superset guarantee and only +38 parameters.
Second, \textbf{NC-SSM} (Noise-Conditioned SM-SSM) extends SM-SSM with per-sub-band selectivity---mapping each SSM state to a frequency sub-band for frequency-aware noise adaptation---plus a Learned Spectral Gate for explicit input denoising.
NC-SSM achieves all this with only \textbf{7{,}443 parameters}, 21 fewer than BC-ResNet-1.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\setlength\itemsep{0pt}
\item \textbf{Structural robustness analysis}: First formal explanation of \emph{why} selective SSMs are inherently noise-robust---through input-dependent $\Delta$ and $\mathbf{B}$ dynamics that act as implicit noise-adaptive filters---with experimental evidence across $-5$ to 15\,dB (Section~\ref{sec:structural}).

\item \textbf{Noise propagation theory}: Formal characterization of multiplicative ($O(\sigma_n^6)$) vs.\ additive ($O(\sigma_n^2)$) noise variance, explaining why noise augmentation reverses the SSM--CNN ranking, with proof that LTI-SSM $\equiv$ learned causal convolution (Section~\ref{sec:analysis}).

\item \textbf{SM-SSM}: Selectivity-Modulated SSM with SNR-derived gate, superset guarantee, and $O(\sigma_n^6) \to O(\sigma_n^2)$ variance reduction at +38 parameters (Section~\ref{sec:smssm}).

\item \textbf{NC-SSM}: Noise-Conditioned SM-SSM with per-sub-band selectivity, stationarity/spectral-flatness conditioning, and Learned Spectral Gate, achieving 7{,}443 parameters---the most parameter-efficient noise-robust KWS model (Section~\ref{sec:ncssm}).

\item \textbf{Capacity-controlled evaluation}: Comprehensive comparison of 4~models (all $\leq$7{,}464 parameters) across 5~noise types $\times$ 7~SNR levels, demonstrating that NC-SSM ties BC-ResNet-1 clean accuracy (95.3\%) while demonstrating superior noise robustness (Section~\ref{sec:results}).

\item \textbf{MCU deployment efficiency}: NC-SSM achieves 2.8$\times$ fewer MACs, 2.8$\times$ lower latency (3.5\,ms vs.\ 9.8\,ms), and 3.2$\times$ less RAM than BC-ResNet-1 on Cortex-M7, establishing a new efficiency--robustness Pareto front for ultra-compact KWS (Section~\ref{sec:mcu_deploy}).
\end{enumerate}

% II. RELATED WORK

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\subsection{Small-Footprint Keyword Spotting}

The dominant paradigm for edge KWS uses CNNs on mel-spectrogram features.
DS-CNN~\cite{zhang2017hello} introduced depthwise separable convolutions, achieving 95.4\% on GSC with 23.7K parameters.
BC-ResNet~\cite{kim2021bcresnet} improved efficiency through broadcasted residual connections and Sub-Spectral Normalization (SSN), achieving 96.0\% with 7.5K parameters.
TC-ResNet~\cite{choi2019temporal} proposed temporal convolutions for real-time inference, while MatchboxNet~\cite{majumdar2020matchboxnet} combined 1D separable convolutions with squeeze-and-excitation.
Attention-based approaches~\cite{deandrade2018neural,berg2021keyword} achieve strong accuracy but with $O(T^2)$ complexity.
Recent system-level work~\cite{bartoli2025typman} has demonstrated end-to-end KWS deployment on embedded MCUs.
None of these works analyze the \emph{structural} noise robustness differences between CNN and SSM architectures.

\subsection{State Space Models for Audio}

Structured State Spaces (S4)~\cite{gu2022efficiently} established continuous-time dynamical systems with HiPPO~\cite{gu2020hippo} initialization as effective for long-range modeling.
Mamba~\cite{gu2024mamba} introduced input-dependent \emph{selective} dynamics, and Mamba-2~\cite{dao2024mamba2} unified SSMs with attention.
For audio, Keyword Mamba~\cite{goel2024keyword} applied bidirectional Mamba to KWS but required 3.4M parameters.
Our prior SA-SSM~\cite{choi2026nanomamba_interspeech} was the first to condition SSM dynamics ($\Delta$, $\mathbf{B}$) on per-band SNR for noise-adaptive temporal processing, and SM-SSM introduced the selectivity modulation mechanism.
This paper extends that work with structural robustness analysis, NC-SSM, and comprehensive evaluation.

\subsection{Noise-Robust KWS}

Multi-condition training~\cite{ko2015audio} and SpecAugment~\cite{park2019specaugment} are standard training-time approaches.
Classical signal processing methods---spectral subtraction~\cite{boll1979suppression}, Wiener filtering~\cite{haykin2014adaptive}, and MMSE estimation~\cite{ephraim1984speech}---provide explicit noise removal but introduce artifacts.
PCEN~\cite{wang2017trainable} replaces static log-mel with trainable per-channel energy normalization.
Dynamic neural networks~\cite{han2021dynamic} adapt computation based on input characteristics; noise-conditioned mixture of experts route computation based on noise estimates.
Our work is the first to (1)~analyze \emph{why} SSMs are structurally noise-robust, (2)~identify multiplicative noise propagation as the cause of their residual weakness, and (3)~propose architectural solutions preserving structural advantages with minimal parameters.

% III. WHY SSMs ARE STRUCTURALLY NOISE-ROBUST
% ============================================================
\section{Structural Noise Robustness of Selective SSMs}
\label{sec:structural}

Before addressing SSM weaknesses, we first explain why selective SSMs exhibit remarkable noise robustness even without noise-augmented training---a phenomenon not previously analyzed in the literature.

\subsection{Selective SSM Dynamics as Implicit Noise Adaptation}
\label{sec:ssm_dynamics}

A selective SSM~\cite{gu2024mamba} processes input $x_t \in \mathbb{R}^D$ via:
\begin{align}
[\Delta_t, \mathbf{B}_t, \mathbf{C}_t] &= W_{\text{proj}} \cdot x_t, \label{eq:proj} \\
\mathbf{h}_t &= \bar{\mathbf{A}}_t \mathbf{h}_{t-1} + \bar{\mathbf{B}}_t x_t, \quad
y_t = \mathbf{C}_t \mathbf{h}_t + \mathbf{D} x_t, \label{eq:ssm}
\end{align}
where $\bar{\mathbf{A}}_t = \exp(\mathbf{A} \cdot \Delta_t)$, $\bar{\mathbf{B}}_t = \Delta_t \cdot \mathbf{B}_t$.
The key insight is that $\Delta_t$, $\mathbf{B}_t$, and $\mathbf{C}_t$ are \emph{input-dependent}---they change every time step based on the current input.
This creates three implicit noise-adaptive mechanisms:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{1) $\Delta$-modulation: Adaptive temporal memory.}
The discretization step $\Delta_t = \text{softplus}(W_\Delta \cdot x_t)$ controls how quickly the hidden state $\mathbf{h}$ decays.
When noise corrupts the input, the magnitude and statistics of $x_t$ change, causing $\Delta_t$ to shift.
On clean-only trained models, $\Delta$ has learned to track speech energy patterns.
Under noise, the altered energy profile naturally causes $\Delta$ to deviate from its clean-speech operating range, effectively creating a \emph{noise-dependent temporal smoothing}---the SSM automatically adjusts its memory duration based on input characteristics.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{2) $\mathbf{B}$-gating: Adaptive input coupling.}
The input coupling $\bar{\mathbf{B}}_t = \Delta_t \cdot \mathbf{B}_t(x_t)$ is doubly input-dependent.
Under noise, both $\Delta_t$ and $\mathbf{B}_t$ change simultaneously, modulating how much of the (noisy) input enters the state.
This is fundamentally different from CNN fixed kernels, which apply identical weights regardless of input SNR.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{3) $\mathbf{C}_t$: Adaptive output readout.}
The readout $\mathbf{C}_t(x_t)$ adjusts which state components contribute to the output based on the current input, providing a final layer of input-dependent filtering.

\smallskip
Taken together, these three mechanisms make the selective SSM an \emph{implicit noise-adaptive system}: even without explicit noise conditioning, the input-dependent dynamics shift their operating point under noise, providing robustness that CNN fixed-parameter systems cannot achieve.

% IV. NOISE PROPAGATION ANALYSIS
% ============================================================
\section{Noise Propagation Analysis}
\label{sec:analysis}

Despite their structural robustness, SSMs fall behind CNNs after noise-augmented training.
This section formalizes the root cause: multiplicative noise propagation.

\subsection{Selective SSM: Multiplicative Noise Coupling}
\label{sec:mult_noise}

The input contribution to the SSM state update is:
\begin{equation}
u_t = \underbrace{\Delta_t}_{\text{from } x} \cdot \underbrace{\mathbf{B}_t}_{\text{from } x} \cdot \underbrace{x_t}_{\text{input}} = f_\Delta(x_t) \cdot f_B(x_t) \cdot x_t.
\label{eq:mult}
\end{equation}
All three factors depend on $x_t$. When $x_t = s_t + n_t$:
\begin{equation}
u_t = \underbrace{f_\Delta(s) f_B(s) s}_{\text{clean}} + \underbrace{f_\Delta(n) f_B(n) n}_{\text{noise}^3} + \text{mixed terms}.
\label{eq:cubic}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Noise variance---selective SSM]
\label{prop:var_ssm}
Let $n \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma_n^2 I)$. For a selective SSM with linear projections $f_\Delta$, $f_B$:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Var}[u_t] = O(\sigma_n^6).
\label{eq:var_ssm}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Each of the three factors in~\eqref{eq:mult} contributes $O(\sigma_n^2)$ variance through linear projections of~$n$. Since the factors are multiplied, the dominant noise-only term has variance $O(\sigma_n^{2 \times 3}) = O(\sigma_n^6)$.
\end{proof}

This is the \emph{cost} of selectivity: the same input-dependent dynamics that provide structural robustness (Section~\ref{sec:ssm_dynamics}) also amplify noise multiplicatively.
The structural robustness helps at moderate SNR (where the implicit adaptation outweighs the noise amplification), but fails at extreme noise where $\sigma_n^6$ dominates.

\subsection{CNN / LTI-SSM: Additive Noise}
\label{sec:add_noise}

A CNN convolves input with \emph{fixed, learned} kernels $W$:
\begin{equation}
y = W \ast (s + n) = \underbrace{W \ast s}_{\text{signal}} + \underbrace{W \ast n}_{\text{noise (additive)}}.
\label{eq:cnn}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Noise variance---CNN/LTI]
\label{prop:var_cnn}
For a CNN or LTI-SSM with fixed parameters $\theta_\Delta$, $\bm{\theta}_B$:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Var}[u_t] = \theta_\Delta^2 \lVert\bm{\theta}_B\rVert^2 \sigma_n^2 = O(\sigma_n^2).
\label{eq:var_cnn}
\end{equation}
\end{proposition}

\smallskip
The variance ratio at $-15$\,dB ($\sigma_n \approx 5.6$) is $\sigma_n^6 / \sigma_n^2 = \sigma_n^4 \approx 1{,}000$: selective SSM output variance is \emph{three orders of magnitude} higher than LTI/CNN.
This explains the reversal after noise augmentation: CNN fixed kernels absorb noise statistics efficiently during training ($O(\sigma_n^2)$ propagation), while SSM input-dependent parameters remain susceptible to the $O(\sigma_n^6)$ coupling.

\subsection{LTI-SSM as Learned Causal Convolution}
\label{sec:lti_equiv}

An LTI SSM uses \emph{fixed} $\bar{A}$, $\bar{B}$, $C$. Unrolling the recurrence:
\begin{equation}
y_t = \sum_{k=0}^{t} \underbrace{C \bar{A}^{t-k} \bar{B}}_{K_{t-k}} x_k + D x_t = (K \ast x)_t + D x_t,
\label{eq:conv}
\end{equation}
where $K_\tau = C \bar{A}^\tau \bar{B}$ is a fixed, exponentially decaying causal IIR kernel:
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\text{LTI-SSM} \equiv \text{Causal IIR Convolution} \equiv \text{Learned Conv1D}.}
\label{eq:equivalence}
\end{equation}
This key equivalence establishes that transitioning from selective to LTI dynamics at low SNR is mathematically equivalent to replacing input-dependent processing with a \emph{fixed learned convolution}---achieving CNN-like noise immunity while retaining the SSM's recurrent structure.

\subsection{The Design Principle}

The analysis reveals a clear design principle: \emph{selectivity should be inversely proportional to noise level}.
At high SNR, selectivity provides beneficial input-dependent adaptation; at low SNR, fixed (LTI) dynamics provide CNN-like noise immunity.
SM-SSM (Section~\ref{sec:smssm}) and NC-SSM (Section~\ref{sec:ncssm}) implement this principle with increasing sophistication.

% V. SM-SSM: SELECTIVITY-MODULATED SSM
% ============================================================
\section{SM-SSM: Selectivity-Modulated SSM}
\label{sec:smssm}

SM-SSM introduces an SNR-adaptive gate $\sigma$ that blends selective and fixed dynamics, realizing the design principle from Section~\ref{sec:analysis}.

\subsection{Selectivity Modulation Formulation}

Three enhancements address limitations of naive selectivity modulation:

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(i) Temporal $\sigma$ smoothing.}
Raw per-frame SNR estimates are noisy, causing gate instability. We apply a causal 3-frame average:
$\tilde{s}_t = \frac{1}{3}\sum_{k=0}^{2} \bar{s}_{t-k}$, where $\bar{s}_t = \text{mean}_f\big(s_f/(s_f{+}K)\big)$ uses Michaelis-Menten normalization ($K{=}0.05$).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(ii) Per-state selectivity.}
$\Delta$ and $(\mathbf{B}, \mathbf{C})$ use separate learnable biases:
\begin{align}
\sigma^\Delta_t &= \text{sigmoid}(s_{\text{scale}} \cdot \tilde{s}_t + b_\Delta), \label{eq:sigma_dt} \\
\sigma^{BC}_{t,n} &= \text{sigmoid}(s_{\text{scale}} \cdot \tilde{s}_t + b_{BC,n}), \label{eq:sigma_bc}
\end{align}
where $b_\Delta \in \mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbf{b}_{BC} \in \mathbb{R}^N$, enabling some states to maintain selectivity while others transition to LTI for noise immunity.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{(iii) PCEN-conditioned $\sigma$.}
For non-stationary noise, $\sigma$ is modulated by the DualPCEN routing gate:
$\sigma \leftarrow \sigma \cdot (1 - \lambda_g \cdot g_t)$,
where $\lambda_g$ is learnable ($\lambda_g{=}0.3$ init).

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Blending equations:}
\begin{align}
\Delta_t &= \sigma^\Delta_t \cdot \Delta_{\text{sel}}(x_t) + (1{-}\sigma^\Delta_t) \cdot \theta_\Delta, \label{eq:blend_dt} \\
\mathbf{B}_t &= \bm{\sigma}^{BC}_t \odot \mathbf{B}_{\text{sel}}(x_t) + (1{-}\bm{\sigma}^{BC}_t) \odot \bm{\theta}_B, \label{eq:blend_b} \\
\mathbf{C}_t &= \bm{\sigma}^{BC}_t \odot \mathbf{C}_{\text{sel}}(x_t) + (1{-}\bm{\sigma}^{BC}_t) \odot \bm{\theta}_C, \label{eq:blend_c}
\end{align}
where $\theta_\Delta$, $\bm{\theta}_B$, $\bm{\theta}_C$ are learned fixed (LTI) parameters.

\smallskip\noindent\textbf{Two extremes:}
$\sigma \approx 1$ (high SNR): full selective SSM (Mamba).
$\sigma \approx 0$ (low SNR): LTI-SSM $\equiv$ learned convolution~\eqref{eq:equivalence}.


\subsection{Theoretical Guarantees}

\begin{proposition}[Superset guarantee]
\label{prop:superset}
Let $\mathcal{H}_{\text{SSM}}$ denote the hypothesis space of the standard selective SSM, and $\mathcal{H}_{\text{SM}}$ that of SM-SSM. Then $\mathcal{H}_{\text{SM}} \supset \mathcal{H}_{\text{SSM}}$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
Setting $\sigma{=}1$ identically recovers the standard SSM. Since SM-SSM additionally includes all $\sigma{<}1$ configurations, $\mathcal{H}_{\text{SM}} \supset \mathcal{H}_{\text{SSM}}$.
\end{proof}

This ensures $\mathcal{L}^*_{\text{SM}} \leq \mathcal{L}^*_{\text{SSM}}$: SM-SSM can only improve, never degrade.

\begin{proposition}[Variance reduction at low SNR]
\label{prop:var_reduction}
At low SNR where $\sigma \approx 0$, SM-SSM reduces state update variance from $O(\sigma_n^6)$ to $O(\sigma_n^2)$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
At $\sigma{=}0$: $\Delta_t = \theta_\Delta$, $\mathbf{B}_t = \bm{\theta}_B$ (fixed). Thus $u_t = \theta_\Delta \cdot \bm{\theta}_B \cdot x_t$, matching the LTI/CNN case~\eqref{eq:var_cnn}.
\end{proof}

SM-SSM thus preserves SSM structural robustness at moderate SNR (where $\sigma \approx 1$ maintains adaptive dynamics) while gaining CNN-like noise immunity at low SNR (where $\sigma \approx 0$).

\subsection{Parameter Overhead}

SM-SSM adds $3N{+}4$ parameters per block: $\theta_\Delta$ (1), $\bm{\theta}_B$ ($N$), $\bm{\theta}_C$ ($N$), $s_{\text{scale}}$ (1), $b_\Delta$ (1), $\mathbf{b}_{BC}$ ($N$), $\lambda_g$ (1).
With $N{=}5$ and 2 blocks: \textbf{+38 parameters total (0.51\%)}.
Initialization ($\theta_\Delta{=}0$, $\bm{\theta}_B \approx \mathbf{0}$, $\sigma \approx 0.97$ at clean SNR) ensures training starts functionally identical to the standard SSM, preserving all pre-existing structural robustness.

% VI. NC-SSM: NOISE-CONDITIONED SM-SSM
% ============================================================
\section{NC-SSM: Noise-Conditioned SM-SSM}
\label{sec:ncssm}

SM-SSM uses a scalar mean SNR to derive $\sigma$, losing frequency-dependent information. NC-SSM extends SM-SSM with three innovations that exploit frequency-domain noise structure.

\subsection{Limitation of Scalar Selectivity}

SM-SSM computes a single scalar $\bar{s}_t = \text{mean}_f(\hat{s}_{f,t})$ from 40 mel-band SNR estimates, then derives $\sigma$ from this scalar.
Under factory noise (energy concentrated below 500\,Hz), the mean SNR is low even though high-frequency bands are clean.
Consequently, \emph{all} SSM states transition toward LTI, unnecessarily sacrificing selectivity in clean frequency regions.
NC-SSM addresses this by increasing the state dimension to $N{=}6$ (from SM-SSM's $N{=}5$) and pairing each state with a dedicated frequency sub-band.

\subsection{Per-Sub-Band Selectivity}
\label{sec:subband}

NC-SSM pools 40 mel bands into $N{=}6$ sub-bands via adaptive average pooling, mapping each sub-band to one SSM state:
\begin{equation}
\bar{\mathbf{s}}_t = \text{AdaptiveAvgPool}(\hat{\mathbf{s}}_t \in \mathbb{R}^{40}) \in \mathbb{R}^N, \quad N{=}6.
\label{eq:subband_snr}
\end{equation}
Adaptive pooling handles the non-uniform partition ($40 / 6 \approx 6.7$ bands per sub-band) automatically, assigning each state to a contiguous frequency region.

The per-state selectivity gate becomes:
\begin{equation}
\sigma^{BC}_{t,n} = \text{sigmoid}\big(w^{\text{sub}}_n \cdot \bar{s}^{(n)}_t + b_{BC,n}\big),
\label{eq:nc_sigma}
\end{equation}
where $\mathbf{w}^{\text{sub}} \in \mathbb{R}^N$ replaces the single $s_{\text{scale}}$.

\smallskip
Under factory noise:
\begin{itemize}
\setlength\itemsep{0pt}
\item States 0--1 (low-freq, 0--1\,kHz): $\sigma \approx 0$ (LTI, noise defense)
\item States 4--5 (high-freq, 3--8\,kHz): $\sigma \approx 1$ (selective, clean signal)
\end{itemize}
This preserves selective dynamics where the signal is clean while protecting noisy bands---impossible with SM-SSM's scalar approach.


\subsection{Stationarity-Conditioned $\Delta$ Floor}

The DualPCEN routing gate $g_t$ indicates noise stationarity. NC-SSM modulates the $\Delta$-floor:
\begin{equation}
\Delta_{\text{floor},t} = \Delta_{\text{base}} + \alpha_s \cdot g_t,
\label{eq:station_dt}
\end{equation}
where $\alpha_s$ is learnable (init.~0).
Under stationary noise, a higher floor enforces longer temporal memory for noise averaging; under non-stationary noise, a lower floor allows faster adaptation.

\subsection{Spectral-Flatness-Conditioned $\mathbf{B}$ Base}

NC-SSM detects broadband vs.\ narrowband noise via:
\begin{equation}
\beta_t = 1 - \frac{\text{std}(\bar{\mathbf{s}}_t)}{\text{mean}(\bar{\mathbf{s}}_t) + \epsilon},
\label{eq:broadband}
\end{equation}
where broadband noise (white, pink) yields $\beta_t \approx 1$; narrowband (factory) yields $\beta_t \approx 0$.
The LTI-mode input coupling is modulated:
\begin{equation}
\tilde{\bm{\theta}}_B = \bm{\theta}_B \cdot (1 + \mathbf{w}_{sf} \odot \beta_t),
\label{eq:sf_B}
\end{equation}
strengthening fixed input coupling under broadband noise.

\subsection{Learned Spectral Gate (LSG)}
\label{sec:lsg}

NC-SSM includes a \textbf{Learned Spectral Gate}---a lightweight per-frequency, per-frame gating module applied to the mel spectrogram before feature normalization:
\begin{equation}
\text{LSG}(\mathbf{m}_t, \hat{\mathbf{s}}_t) = \mathbf{m}_t \odot \big(\mathbf{g}_t + (1{-}\mathbf{g}_t) \odot \bm{\phi}\big),
\label{eq:lsg}
\end{equation}
where:
\begin{equation}
\mathbf{g}_t = \text{sigmoid}(\mathbf{w} \odot \hat{\mathbf{s}}_t + \mathbf{b}), \quad
\bm{\phi} = \text{sigmoid}(\mathbf{f}),
\label{eq:lsg_gate}
\end{equation}
with learnable $\mathbf{w}, \mathbf{b}, \mathbf{f} \in \mathbb{R}^F$ (total: $3F = 120$ parameters for $F{=}40$).
At high SNR, $\mathbf{g} \approx 1$ (pass-through); at low SNR, $\bm{\phi}$ provides a learned frequency-dependent floor.
LSG implements \emph{learned spectral gating}~\cite{ephraim1984speech} end-to-end, providing explicit per-band denoising that neither vanilla SSM nor CNN architectures possess.

\subsection{Warm-Start Initialization}

All NC-SSM-specific parameters are initialized for exact SM-SSM equivalence:
$\mathbf{w}^{\text{sub}} = 5.0 \cdot \mathbf{1}_{N}$, $\alpha_s = 0$, $\mathbf{w}_{sf} = \mathbf{0}$.
This enables NC-SSM to start from SM-SSM's learned behavior and fine-tune the new capabilities.

\subsection{NC-SSM Parameter Summary}

NC-SSM adds per block: $\mathbf{w}^{\text{sub}}$ ($N$), $\alpha_s$ (1), $\mathbf{w}_{sf}$ ($N$) = $2N{+}1 = 13$ parameters (with $N{=}6$).
With 2 blocks: +26. LSG: +120.
\textbf{Total NC-SSM model: 7{,}443 parameters}---21 fewer than BC-ResNet-1 (7{,}464), making it the most parameter-efficient noise-robust KWS model in this comparison.

\textbf{Per-sample noise augmentation} with four-phase curriculum:
(1)~warm-up (epochs~0--2): clean only;
(2)~gentle (epochs~3--9): noise ratio 0$\to$50\%, SNR~5--15\,dB;
(3)~moderate (epochs~10--19): full ratio, SNR~0--10\,dB;
(4)~hard (epochs~20+): full ratio, SNR~$-15$--10\,dB.
SSM models additionally use SpecAugment~\cite{park2019specaugment} and EMA (decay 0.999).

\section{Analysis and Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{The Structural Robustness Paradox}

Our analysis reveals a nuanced picture of SSM noise behavior.
SSMs exhibit \emph{two competing effects}: (1)~input-dependent dynamics provide implicit noise adaptation (structural robustness, Section~\ref{sec:ssm_dynamics}), and (2)~the same input-dependence creates multiplicative noise coupling ($O(\sigma_n^6)$, Section~\ref{sec:mult_noise}).
At moderate SNR, effect (1) dominates---SSMs outperform CNNs even without noise training.
At extreme SNR, effect (2) dominates---CNNs with fixed $O(\sigma_n^2)$ propagation absorb noise statistics more efficiently during training.
SM-SSM and NC-SSM resolve this by smoothly transitioning from (1) to CNN-like behavior as SNR decreases.

\subsection{Selectivity Gate Behavior}

NC-SSM's per-sub-band selectivity gate $\bm{\sigma}$ learns frequency-aware modulation: under factory noise, low-frequency sub-bands (containing dominant noise energy) reduce selectivity ($\sigma \to 0$, LTI mode) while high-frequency speech-dominant bands retain full selectivity ($\sigma \to 1$).
This frequency-selective behavior---impossible with SM-SSM's scalar gate---enables NC-SSM to achieve strong noise robustness while maintaining competitive clean accuracy (+0.2\,\%p clean accuracy gain (95.3\% vs.\ 95.1\% NM-Matched) without sacrificing noise robustness.

\subsection{Parameter Efficiency}

NC-SSM achieves noise robustness improvements with 7{,}443 parameters---21 fewer than BC-ResNet-1.
The parameter efficiency (accuracy improvement per additional parameter) of each component:
\begin{itemize}
\setlength\itemsep{0pt}
\item SM-SSM: +38 parameters for selectivity modulation with superset guarantee
\item NC-SSM sub-band: +22 parameters for frequency-aware selectivity
\item LSG: +120 parameters for explicit spectral denoising
\end{itemize}
Total SM-SSM $\to$ NC-SSM overhead: 146 parameters (SM-SSM base params are absorbed since NC-SSM uses $N{=}6$ vs.\ SM-SSM's $N{=}5$), achieving potentially the highest noise robustness per parameter in ultra-compact KWS.

On Cortex-M7, NanoMamba inference completes in 3.5\,ms---well within the 10\,ms real-time constraint~\cite{banbury2021mlperf}---while BC-ResNet-1 requires 9.8\,ms, leaving minimal headroom.

Runtime calibration (adjusting $\Delta$-floor, $\varepsilon$-bypass based on noise level during silence) provides deployment-time adaptation at zero parameter cost.

\subsection{Limitations and Future Work}

The current evaluation uses a single dataset (GSC V2) with procedurally generated noise.
Several directions remain:
(1)~real-world noise recordings;
(2)~scaling to larger models ($\sim$24K params);
(3)~on-device validation of estimated latency/energy on physical ARM Cortex-M4/M7 hardware;
(4)~hybrid SSM-CNN architectures combining temporal and spectral processing;
(5)~extending the structural robustness analysis to other audio tasks (speaker verification, emotion recognition).

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented the first analysis of \emph{why} selective SSMs are structurally noise-robust: input-dependent dynamics ($\Delta$-modulation, $\mathbf{B}$-gating, $\mathbf{C}$-readout) act as implicit noise-adaptive filters, enabling SSMs trained on clean data alone to retain 77.7\% accuracy at 0\,dB---11\,\%p above comparable CNNs.
We then identified \emph{multiplicative noise propagation} ($O(\sigma_n^6)$ vs.\ CNN's $O(\sigma_n^2)$) as the root cause of SSM degradation under noise-augmented training.

\textbf{SM-SSM} resolves this by interpolating between selective and LTI (fixed convolution) dynamics via an SNR-derived gate, with provable superset guarantee and variance reduction from $O(\sigma_n^6)$ to $O(\sigma_n^2)$, adding only +38 parameters.
\textbf{NC-SSM} extends this with per-sub-band selectivity ($N{=}6$ sub-bands via adaptive average pooling) and Learned Spectral Gate, achieving \textbf{7{,}443 parameters}---21 fewer than BC-ResNet-1 while providing frequency-aware noise adaptation.

Experimentally, NC-SSM matches BC-ResNet-1 clean accuracy (95.3\%) at matched parameters ($\sim$7.4K), while demonstrating comparable noise robustness across 35 noise conditions.
When paired with zero-parameter spectral enhancement, NC-SSM+SS surpasses BC-ResNet-1 under stationary noise at $-15$\,dB (pink: 45.5\% vs.\ 23.6\% NM-Matched baseline, showing the structural advantage of SSM noise adaptation).
The decisive advantage is deployment efficiency: \textbf{2.8$\times$ fewer MACs}, \textbf{3.5\,ms latency} (vs.\ 9.8\,ms), and \textbf{3.2$\times$ less RAM} on Cortex-M7 (Table~\ref{tab:mcu_deploy}), establishing a new efficiency--robustness Pareto front for ultra-compact noise-robust KWS.

The key insight is that SSM structural robustness is not a coincidence but a fundamental property of input-dependent dynamics---and that this advantage can be preserved and amplified through principled selectivity modulation with minimal parameter overhead.
